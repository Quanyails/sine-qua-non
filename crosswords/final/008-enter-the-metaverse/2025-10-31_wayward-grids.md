Commentary:

> Fun fact: This was the first piece of feedback I received from a crossword outlet!
> 
> With 75 theme cells, I knew that this grid was ambitious. At the same time, though, this made it the least committal of my grids to send off to a major outlet. My primary goal with submitting this grid was to get a pulse on what outlets were looking for, beyond what I could learn from studying resources and receiving playtesting feedback.
>
> I think Universal delivered on the feedback I was hoping to receive. Upon reading the rejection email, I got some helpful takeaways, including:
> 
> - Make the crossword just as much fun to solve for as it is to construct. You might have a really fun theme you want to lay out onto a grid, but the theme might end up feeling repetitive as a solver.
> - Entries some people consider to be assets may not tide over the same way to another publication. For example, FIVE WS has a score of 60 on the XWord Info word list as of writing, but this entry was specifically pointed out by Universal as a ding.
> - Prioritize theme and enjoyment over technical quality. The grid I submitted uses 28 three-letter words, which is on the high side. I was concerned whether the number of three-letter words was of concern, which I called out in my initial email. Universal did not draw attention to this in their feedback, instead focusing on puzzle elements with higher pertinence.
