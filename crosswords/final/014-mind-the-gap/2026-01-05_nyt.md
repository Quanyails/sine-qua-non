# NY Times Submission

(Unused: The New York Times has occasionally seen crosswords that employ blank squares in their gimmicks. Some of these crosswords place blank squares in horizontal rows, one-square rebuses, Schrodinger's tiles, or at the edge of the grid (making them equivalent to black squares). This crossword employs a pinwheel configuration where the blank square is skipped over. It is my hope that this configuration presents a novel "spin" on this gimmick. 😉)
