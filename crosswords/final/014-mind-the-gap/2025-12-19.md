# Mind the Gap

## Design notes

- I went with a pinwheel configuration because it makes this puzzle more "magical". Other NYT crosswords place blank squares in horizontal rows, one-square rebuses, or at the edge of the grid. In contrast, this configuration lets me include blank squares that stretch both horizontally and vertically mid-grid.
- This crossword was submitted as a themed crossword. Really, though, it straddles the line between a themed and unthemed crossword, as only half of the 40 theme squares actually constrain the fill.
- Given how many of the four-letter entries are actually three-letter entries, I made sure the number of three-letter entries remained reasonable after "compression".
- Due to the above, the corners of the grid are technically more closed off than originally perceived.
